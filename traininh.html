Common Poultry Diseases

Coccidiosis

Symptoms: Bloody diarrhea, lethargy, loss of appetite, dehydration

Cause: Parasitic infection by coccidia protozoa

Treatment: Anticoccidial medications, supportive care

Newcastle Disease

Symptoms: Respiratory distress, nervous signs, decreased egg production

Cause: Viral infection (Newcastle disease virus)

Treatment: Supportive care, vaccination programs

Salmonella

Symptoms: Diarrhea, fever, weakness, sudden death in young birds

Cause: Bacterial infection (Salmonella species)

Treatment: Antibiotics, improved hygiene, biosecurity measures

Prevention Best Practices

Biosecurity Measures

  • • Limit visitor access to poultry areas
  • • Disinfect equipment and footwear
  • • Quarantine new birds before introduction
  • • Control rodent and wild bird access

Hygiene Practices

  • • Clean water and feed systems regularly
  • • Remove dead birds promptly
  • • Maintain proper ventilation
  • • Clean and disinfect housing regularly

Vaccination Programs

  • • Follow recommended vaccination schedules
  • • Use appropriate vaccines for your region
  • • Maintain cold chain for vaccines
  • • Monitor antibody levels

Nutrition & Environment

  • • Provide balanced nutrition
  • • Ensure adequate space per bird
  • • Maintain optimal temperature
  • • Monitor stress factors
